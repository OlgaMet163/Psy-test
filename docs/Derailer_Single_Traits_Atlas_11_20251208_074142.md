# Derailer Single Traits Atlas (11 traits)

Development-oriented descriptions of each trait across four levels (Low, Typical, Elevated, High) and eight life domains. This is not a diagnostic instrument.

## **VR — Volatile Reactivity**

**Core stress mechanism:** emotional arousal spikes quickly under stress.

**Under stress you may show:** tone sharpens, patience drops, and reactions come fast.

**Primary derailment risk:** conflict escalates and others experience unpredictability.

### Low

Very even-keeled; may under-signal urgency or seem emotionally flat in situations requiring heat/mobilization.

- **Career:** Low reactivity and steadier affect; may under-communicate urgency or intensity.
- **Business:** Low reactivity and steadier affect; may under-communicate urgency or intensity.
- **Friendships:** Low reactivity and steadier affect; may under-communicate urgency or intensity.
- **Hobbies:** Low reactivity and steadier affect; may under-communicate urgency or intensity.
- **Romantic:** Low reactivity and steadier affect; may under-communicate urgency or intensity.
- **Overall lifestyle:** Low reactivity and steadier affect; may under-communicate urgency or intensity.
- **Health:** Low reactivity and steadier affect; may under-communicate urgency or intensity.
- **Sports:** Low reactivity and steadier affect; may under-communicate urgency or intensity.

### Typical

Responsive and appropriately expressive; can recover after stress and repair quickly.

- **Career:** Balanced expression; can use the trait adaptively without it dominating the situation.
- **Business:** Balanced expression; can use the trait adaptively without it dominating the situation.
- **Friendships:** Balanced expression; can use the trait adaptively without it dominating the situation.
- **Hobbies:** Balanced expression; can use the trait adaptively without it dominating the situation.
- **Romantic:** Balanced expression; can use the trait adaptively without it dominating the situation.
- **Overall lifestyle:** Balanced expression; can use the trait adaptively without it dominating the situation.
- **Health:** Balanced expression; can use the trait adaptively without it dominating the situation.
- **Sports:** Balanced expression; can use the trait adaptively without it dominating the situation.

### Elevated

Irritability and intensity rise; faster escalation and slower recovery under sustained pressure.

- **Career:** tone spikes, defensiveness to feedback, performance variance across weeks.
- **Business:** rapid pivots and escalation culture; urgency communicated with heat.
- **Friendships:** intensity cycles; sensitivity to slights; rupture–repair patterns.
- **Hobbies:** frustration spikes; quits/pauses abruptly or pushes angrily.
- **Romantic:** arguments ignite quickly; push–pull closeness; fast escalation.
- **Overall lifestyle:** bursts and crashes; routines swing with mood and stress.
- **Health:** sleep disruption/tension; coping volatility (comfort behaviors or overcontrol).
- **Sports:** overreaches after setbacks; heat with coaches/partners; volatile load choices.

Explosive or unpredictable affect; others adapt by avoiding candor; repeated rupture–repair failures.

- **Career:** tone spikes, defensiveness to feedback, performance variance across weeks; stronger frequency/intensity, lower recovery, and higher downstream consequences.
- **Business:** rapid pivots and escalation culture; urgency communicated with heat; stronger frequency/intensity, lower recovery, and higher downstream consequences.

- **Sports:** overreaches after setbacks; heat with coaches/partners; volatile load choices; stronger frequency/intensity, lower recovery, and higher downstream consequences.
